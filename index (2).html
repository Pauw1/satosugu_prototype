The space between two breaths

Suguru had learned, over the years, that silence between them was never empty. It carried weight — sometimes the weight of an unspoken question, sometimes the weight of an answer neither of them was ready to give. Tonight, on the rooftop of the dormitory, the silence had teeth.

Satoru sat at the edge, legs swinging into the dark, the city humming below them in patches of amber and white. His blindfold was gone. He hadn't said why.

"You've been quiet," Suguru said, finally, because someone had to.

"Have I?" Satoru didn't turn. "I thought I was being charming."
